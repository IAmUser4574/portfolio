The best personal sites are small systems, not frozen posters. They help someone understand what you make, how you think, and where to go next.

For this site, the home page starts with four clear destinations: About, Projects, Blog, and CV. The structure is intentionally simple because simple things survive iteration.

## The Site As A Workbench

I want this homebase to carry current work without needing a redesign every time a project changes shape. Projects can be swapped in, essays can be added as MDX, and the CV can stay readable without becoming a PDF maze.

That makes the site useful to visitors, but it also makes it useful to maintain. Good personal infrastructure should make the next update easier.

## What Comes Next

The next layer is richer project case studies: goals, tradeoffs, screenshots, and notes on what changed after real usage. The blog can then hold the thinking that does not belong inside a project page.
