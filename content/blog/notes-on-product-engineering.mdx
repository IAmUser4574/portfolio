Product engineering is the habit of holding two truths at once: the code has to be right, and the experience has to make sense to someone who does not care how the code works.

That means technical taste matters, but so does pacing. The job is often to find the smallest reliable version of the idea, ship it cleanly, and learn from the surface area it exposes.

## Useful Defaults

- Make the core action obvious.
- Keep data shapes boring until the domain proves otherwise.
- Prefer visible progress over invisible cleverness.
- Leave room for the product to tell you what it wants to become.

## The Craft Part

Polish is not decoration. Polish is what happens when edge cases, loading states, copy, spacing, and navigation all agree about what the user is trying to do.

The nicest tools tend to feel calm because somebody did the hard work of deciding what not to make the user think about.
